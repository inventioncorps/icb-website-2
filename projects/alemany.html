Background

We worked on a mobile application to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of work done by volunteers on Alemany Farm, specifically by improving their access to and ability to record information about the trees they work with. In doing so, we provided the tools for long-term sustainability to the farm.

Our partner

Alemany Farm is a non-profit urban farm based in San Francisco that provides free, fresh, and nutritious produce to communities who cannot otherwise access it. It aims to grow food security and educate local residents about how they can produce their own food. We worked with Abby Bell, the Farm Manager, and Jack Thomas, the Assistant Farm Manager.

The problem

Without a standardized procedure for data collection and no central information distribution system, volunteers at Alemany Farm did not have the necessary tools to document the needs of the trees they were working with. Improving this process would lead to greater efficiency and ensure the sustainability of the farm as an organization.

Who is our user?

Farm Volunteers

who currently cannot access or store information about the trees they are working with

Our users need...

Easy access to information about trees in the Alemany Farm orchards.
A standardized method for recording information about trees and their treatments.
To spend their volunteer time in the most efficient manner possible.

Deliverables

We designed and created a mobile application that allows volunteers to instantly access historical data and information about the trees they work with and provides a simple and intuitive process for data collection.

How might we provide volunteers with accessible information about trees?

After logging in to our app, the landing page allows volunteers to scan a tree’s unique QR code that is linked to the tree's dedicated page within the application. This page includes information about treatments, harvests, and disease symptoms – among other things.
The interactive map of the orchards allows users to navigate to certain trees and to add or remove trees from the system as needed.

The QR code improves the accessibility of information and history about the trees through a direct user interface given the volunteer’s close proximity to a specific tree. The mapping system helps volunteers navigate the orchard more easily.

How might we facilitate a standardized, intuitive data collection process for volunteers?

The app needed to be quickly accessible as well as easily navigable after the care for each tree was completed. Efficiency and ease of use was key.

Our data input page includes the following features that make standardized, simple record-keeping a possibility for volunteers: horizontal scrolling, overlaid pop-up menus for inputting new data, and an automatic time-stamp for each entry all assist with this process.

Historical data can be analyzed using the data visualization feature. This can be paired with rainfall and other weather data from local weather stations to better understand the relationships between a variety of factors and quantity of food produced by a specific tree.

How might we create an integrated branding campaign that communicates the connection and purpose between both programs and the social impact on students?

Helping volunteers maximize the time they spend working on productive tasks was a key goal of ours. For this reason, our mobile application needed to be simple, clear, and intuitive for our volunteers.

We put careful thought into the typography of the app, the colors, and their meanings, and the overall positioning of key elements to ensure we maintained a sophisticated aesthetic while creating an intuitive interactive experience.

A light green was used for primary buttons that are associated with positive actions whereas a deep red was used for secondary buttons and flags that alert the user or highlight important information. We designed fruit icons for the individual pages of each type of tree in the orchard.
