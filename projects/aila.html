Background

We partnered with Aila Health on redesigning their physician and patient platforms. The purpose of these platforms is to allow remote monitoring of patients suffering from chronic illnesses to better understand and diagnose their conditions.

Our partner

Aila Health is on a mission to bring personalized care to each and every patient. They are using artificial intelligence to empower healthcare providers and patients to make better healthcare decisions. Aila Health helps chronic illness patients manage all of their conditions in one place while simultaneously helping providers maximize reimbursement with their easy-to-use remote patient monitoring platform.

The problem

The current state of the physician and patient platforms are not ideal. The physician platform needs an improvement in informational hierarchy and sequestering of patient data. The prior state of the platform was more confusing the use and some lapses in informational logic. The platform is not being extensively used by physicians yet, so provider feedback is also necessary to discover more pain points in the ongoing redesign.

Who is our user?

Physicians & Care Teams

of patients who suffer from persistent illnesses and chronic conditions

Our users need...

An easy-to-use platform for less technologically savvy providers who want to effectively monitor their patients
Their feedback and opinions on the current state of the platform to be heard and documented.

Deliverables

Our solution to the problem: a platform for physicians to removtely monitor chronically ill patients to better understand their unique conditions. We co-designed the platform through the work of an interview team and a wireframing team that maximized potential such that the app could utilize as much physician feedback as possible. It’s intuitive and organized, yet powerful enough to accomplish the needs of the physicians who would be using it.

Figma Wireframe

How might we create a physician platform that is intuitive and simple, yet informative and useful?

We wanted to create a wireframe that had Aila’s target demographic in mind: older physicians who wanted something that woudn’t be too complicated but still had functionality.

To accomplish this, we reorganized the original platform to include sections, each with their own individual bubbles for ease fo navigation.

Our app consists of:

  • A redesign of the original aesthetic
  • Reorganized sections consiting of Patient Info, Care Plan, and Body System
  • Reimagined way to track different metrics such as exercise or diet

User Interviews

How might we implement user feedback into our design for the health provider interface?

In our user interviews of healthcare providers, we gained a better understanding of:

  • Useful features and shortcuts
  • The importance of user-friendly design and training for healthcare providers who are not well-versed in technology
  • Minimizing delays
  • The need for proper emphasis of certain features

The 5-second rule is crucial — a physician must be able to come to a conclusion when looking at data, tables, or graphs within 5 seconds. Following the 5-second rule, it is critical that designers make simple, comprehensive designs for professionals using the platform that are not technologically experienced.
